Lynda Brand Notes

What is a brand?

  • Comes from the days where you would brand cattle. Then it became something you owned and stood behind.
  • It can manifest in multiple ways
  • Communicates what the business stands for
  • Relationship between business and audience
  • Result of everything you do

How do we make it more powerful?

  • Choose optimal position and let it guide everything you do.
  • Brand Strategy Choosing brand's optimal position.
    • Brand Strategy is the overlap between the customer's desire and your unique business strength.
  • Choosing Brand's Optimal Position Assess where brand fits in customer's world

Frame Your Strategy
The Foundation

Prepare

  1. Define the target customer"
    • Identify uniting theme
    • Explore who you benefit most
    • Determine who you bring most value
    • Consider your mission - why are you in business, who are you in business to serve?
  2. and the competitive frame of reference - "you instead of what?
    • Direct competitor
    • Substitute
    • Workaround
    • Something completely different
  3. Schedule customer interviews
    • Do personal phone interviews for 30-40 minutes
  4. Prepare questions for those intreviews, an Interview Guide

    Empathy Interviews - Helps you view your business and brand development from the customer's standpoint

    • Start at a high altitude and then narrow down

Listen

Brand Positioning - Make it easy for your customer to buy. Listen to get that data.

  1. Get into a listening mindset
    • Open
    • Genuine
    • Non-judgemental
    • Curious

    Maintaine a Beginner's Mind - Be open to what emerges without being attached to a certain result.

  2. When conducting an interview continually ask yourself why?
  3. What about that matters to you?
  4. Whatelse?
  5. What is important to you here?

Compile

So what did you hear?

  1. Compile all your notes
  2. Create a Customer Profile - Tap into the customer's deeply held beliefs and values. Serves as a touchstone for new and old marketing initiatives.
  3. Distill what you've learn for future reference
  4. Define Demographics - describe basics of ideal customer, then expand.
  5. Define Psychographics - Get inside your customer's head
    • their attitudes
    • aspirations
    • worries
  6. Behaviors - Know your customer's baseline behavior and why your customer behaves that way.
  7. Beliefs - Understand beliefs to better influence behaviors, they drive behaviors after all.

Understand what makes them tick, where your brand fits into that, and therefor how you talk about and deliver your offering to them.

Craft Your Brand Promise

Examine

Use the Uncommon Denominator Framework as it synthesizes:

  • What we know about customer
  • What we know about competitor
  • What we know about our strengths
  1. Inventory findings into a Sven Diagram between (from the customer POV)
    • Customer Desires
    • Competitor Strenths
    • Your Strengths
  2. Identify what falls into the overlap of all 3, the Common Denominator
  3. Identify what falls into the overlap between the Customer Desires and Your Strengths - the Uncommon Denominator

Ladder

Keep upping the stakes on what the benefit to the customer is. Establish tiers you release over time with buy-in and being established.

  • Ladder - Prevents overstepping - like, they can't believe it, or it doesn't make sense.
  • It also helps you to see where you're going
  • Profit Margin - The large the emotional benefit, the more customers are willing to pay. So slow roll it.
  • Competitive Moat - the higher you are on the ladder, the harder it is for others to copy.
  • Headroom to Grow - The higher you are on the ladder, the more you can grow laterally.

Create a Benefits Ladder

  • What is that functional benefit your customer will enjoy?
  • How is their day better? What does it enable, at an emotional level?
  • How is their life better? What do they enjoy because of you?

Positioning Statement

Shows you, your teams, and your partners the essence of your brand's position.

In plain words answer:

  • Who is your target customer?
  • What is the name of your brand?
  • What is your customer's frame of reference?
  • What is your brand promise?
  • What are your reasons to believe?
  • What is the customer end reward for having chosen your brand?

Positioning Statement Format

To [your target customer], [your brand's name] is the one [your frame of reference or category] that [brand promise]. That's because only [your brand's name] brings [reason to believe], so that [your target customer] can [end reward].

Example Positioning Statement

Batter Pancakes Positioning Statement

To moms with kids at home, Batter Pancakes is the one pancake mix that brings Swedish deliciousness. That's because only Batter Pancakes brings super thin pancakes with a crispy crunch, using our Swedish grandmother's recipe, so that your family can start their weekend with extraordinary.
